Laura McHugh produces digital media in Washington, D.C.

Video

Curriculum Inspirations is a video series starring James Tanton that was conceptualized and produced while Laura was working at the Mathematical Association of America. In each video, James use his ten established strategies to break down complex problems from MAA's American Mathematics Competitions exams. In addition to shooting and editing over 160 videos for this series, Laura also managed marketing this series to MAA members and the greater math community.
A full playlist is available here.

Connected Classrooms is a Google community that empowers educators to collaborate with one another. In 2014, they formed a partnership with MAA to connect MAA members (graduate and undergraduate-level math professors) with K-12 classrooms across the country. The goal was to During April 2014 Math Awareness Month, four Connected Classrooms occured between college professors and classrooms (the youngest being a kindergarten classroom). Topics covered including mathemagic, math and card tricks, and math and mimes. These were filmed as a live Google Hangout. Implementation, participant training, and logistics were handled by Laura coordinating between staff at Google and MAA members across the country. This partnership resulted in MAA being a featured partner of Google Connected Classrooms and an increase of 200,000 followers on Google Plus.

Foreign Exchange with Daljit Dhaliwal was a weekly foreign affairs talk show on PBS. The premise was a discussion of international affairs with international voices. Laura covered logistics, script-writing, and final video editing using Final Cut Pro.
